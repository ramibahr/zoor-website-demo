Cookie Policy

Last updated: 1 January 2024  |  Zoor Ltd  |  info@houseofzoor.co.uk

This Cookie Policy explains how Zoor Ltd ("ZOOR", "we", "us" or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website. By continuing to use our website, you consent to our use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, tablet or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to the website owner.

Cookies can be "session cookies" (which expire when you close your browser) or "persistent cookies" (which remain on your device for a set period or until you delete them).

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to:

  • Ensure the website functions correctly and securely
  • Remember your cookie consent preference
  • Improve your experience as you navigate the site

3. Types of Cookies We Use

3.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function correctly. They enable core functionality such as security and accessibility, and cannot be switched off. They are set only in response to actions you take, such as setting your privacy preferences.

  • zoor_cookies_accepted — Stores your cookie consent preference. Expires after 12 months. Set by Zoor Ltd.

3.2 Performance and Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies are not currently active on this website. We may introduce analytics tools (such as Google Analytics) in the future to help us understand how visitors interact with our website. If we do, we will update this Cookie Policy and request your consent before setting any analytics cookies.

3.3 Marketing and Targeting Cookies

We do not currently use marketing or targeting cookies. If this changes in the future, we will update this policy and obtain your consent before doing so.

3.4 Functionality Cookies

We may use functionality cookies to remember your preferences and personalise the site for you. These cookies do not track your activity on other websites.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Our website may include content or features served by third parties (such as embedded maps or payment processors). These third parties may set their own cookies on your device. We do not control these cookies and they are subject to the relevant third party's privacy and cookie policies.

Examples of third-party services that may set cookies include:

  • Payment processors (e.g. Stripe, PayPal) — for secure transaction processing
  • Google Fonts — for serving web fonts (may log requests; see Google's Privacy Policy)

5. Your Cookie Choices

5.1 Our Cookie Banner

When you first visit our website, you will be shown a cookie banner. You can choose to accept or decline non-essential cookies at that point. Your preference will be saved for 12 months.

5.2 Browser Settings

You can control and manage cookies through your browser settings. Most browsers allow you to:

  • View cookies that have been set
  • Block all cookies or cookies from specific websites
  • Delete cookies from your device

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website. For guidance on how to manage cookies in your browser, visit:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.3 Opt-Out of Analytics (Future)

If and when we introduce analytics tools, we will provide an opt-out mechanism. The Google Analytics opt-out browser add-on (tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout) can also be used to prevent data collection by Google Analytics.

6. Changes to This Cookie Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time, particularly as we introduce new website features or technologies. We will post the updated policy on this page with an updated date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.

7. More Information

For more information about how we handle your personal data, please see our Privacy Policy.

For information about cookies generally, visit www.allaboutcookies.org.
